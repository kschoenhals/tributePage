Facts about Leonard Nimoy

  • Leonard Simon Nimoy was born on March 26, 1931, in Boston, Massachusetts.
  • At the young age of 17, he took a role of Ralphie for the renowned Clifford Odets’ production, ‘Awake and Sing’.
  • Enrolled at Boston College in 1949 after being involved with several acting projects at Boston English High School.
  • Applied for California’s Pasadena Playhouse where he started to follow Konstantin Stanislavsky’s Method acting concepts.
  • Joined the US Army Reserve in 1953, but only served 18 months.
  • During his services with the military, he directed and even acted in ‘A Streetcar Named Desire’ in co-ordination with the Atlanta Theater Guild.
  • Played Spock in Star Trek: The Original Series from 1966 - 1969.
  • Earned his Masters in Education from Antioch University in 1977.
  • Continued to play Spock in various movies and TV cameos until 2013.
  • Received a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame on January 16th, 1985.
  • Died on February 17th, 2015 from complications due to chronic obstructive pulmonary disease.
  • A 10 km (6.2 mi) wide asteroid, 4864 Nimoy was named after him on June 2, 2015 by the Jet Propulsion Laboratory.
  • His hometown of Boston honors him by having "Leonard Nimoy Day" on November 14th every year.
